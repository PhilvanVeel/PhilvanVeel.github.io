VR automotive research

This individual research project is a study on how haptic vibrational feedforward reduces motion sickness for rear-facing passengers. For this project I made a unity simulation of the context, a physical prototype and designed the study itself.       

DISCOVER

Why rear-facing passengers?  

Several automotive manufacturers have developed concept cars with face-to-face layouts, such as the Renault Symbioz, Mini Urbanaut, Volkswagen Sedric, and the Volvo 360 concept. To fully benefit from driving in an AV, research indicates that it would be optimal to have an alternative seat layout, compared to the regular rows pointing in the same direction. This futuristic seat configuration is an old idea, since the early automobiles that were developed when horse carriages were the main transportation method, featured a similar layout

Haptic vibrational feedforward

Vibrotactile displays are actuators that communicate information by causing a sense of vibration through the perception of touch. When used solely, it functions as a non-distracting, discrete and immediate form of communication. The vibrations can provide occupants with information such as the location of surrounding objects, and the position of the vehicle on the road.

Methodology

a motion sickness study for rear-facing passengers

  • viable qualification methods were used such as MSAQ, MSHQ and SAM
  • An accesible and replicatable method was developed for this research
  • The study indicated a very positive relation between vibrational feedback and motion sickness prevention

Unity 3d VR self driving car simulation

  • A safe way to test motion sickness in self driving cars
  • emersive, realistic rear-facing driving experience
  • perfect replecation for a controlled experiment
  • low cost way of prototyping and researching

Physical working vibrotactile seat

  • vibrates directionally when the simulation makes a turn, based on logged timestamps
  • simple construction allowed multiple iterations
  • tested optimised balance between comfortableness and vibrotactile threshold
`

Conclusions

Motion sickness was reduced significantly in this study.

  • In 10 categories the lowest possible score was achieved.
  • Participants felt more in control and relaxed when vibrotactile feedforward was provided.
  • The MSHQ was proportional to the experienced motion sickness.
  • The results were as expected based on the literature.
  • This study is indicative of the correlation between providing feedforward and motion sickness.
  • The study has its limitation, but the correlation is present.
