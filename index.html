TU/ECOMOTIVE

What is TU/Ecomotive?

Ecomotive is a student team at the University of Technology Eindhoven that innovates in building cars with a low environmental footprint. Where other teams excel at efficiency in driving, Ecomotive focusses on the build process, materials and lifecycle. Often car manufacturers use lightweight composites to increase efficiency, but these materials are toxic, and after the car’s lifecycle has ended, these materials cannot be recycled.

This year's mission'

TU/Ecomotive is an automotive student team, focussing on reducing the CO2 footprint of car manufacturing. ZEM (zero emission mobility) Is a car that has been designed and build in a single academic year. ZEM is progressive thinking, new technologies, cooperation with companies wrapped in a visually appealing, uncompromising package.

We want to change the automotive industry by proving that cars can be manufactured in a more sustainable way, without compromising the design, use and quality of the vehicle. This year we decided to reach for the stars and archive a CO2 neutral car, by employing smart engineering solutions, partnering up with innovative companies and consulting experts in the field. The car is still currently under development.

VR Design Research


Multiple automotive manufacturers have developed concept cars with face-to-face layouts, such as the Renault Symbioz, Mini Urbanaut, Volkswagen Sedric, and the Volvo 360 concept. To fully benefit from driving in an AV, research indicates that it would be optimal to have an alternative seat layout, compared to the regular rows pointing in the same direction. This futuristic seat configuration is an old idea, since the early automobiles that were developed when horse carriages were the main transportation method, featured a similar layout

Vibrotactile displays are actuators that communicate information by causing a sense of vibration through the perception of touch. When used solely, it functions as a non-distracting, discrete and immediate form of communication. The vibrations can provide occupants with information such as the location of surrounding objects, and the position of the vehicle on the road.
