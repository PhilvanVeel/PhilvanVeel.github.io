The evolution of an innovative student team

Meet 'Jane'

TU/ecomotive is a student team at the University of Technology Eindhoven. Every year a new team builds a car from scratch, aiming to be as sustainable as possible when it comes to the production, use and end-of-life cycle. This year we decided to reach for the stars and archive a CO2 neutral car, by employing smart engineering solutions, partnering up with innovative companies and consulting experts in the field. The car is still currently under development.


My Role

As chief design, working closely with the other departments is vital to the success of this car. The goal of ‘Jane’ will be to show alternative ways of production, and to inspire the industry to adopt sustainability in the production and end-of-life cycle. Therefore, it is not only important to be sustainable, but to convey a modern, attractive and sporty concept. Luckily I had the privilage of leading a team of four hard working industrial design students, since many student teams have build innovative and sustainable cars, in the end I was the responsibile person to take sustainability, desirability and innovation to the next level through the language of design.

Teamwork makes the dream work

Being Chief Design is the biggest, most challenging and most rewarding design experience iencountered yet. Working with an interdiscplinary team of over 30 students poses a lot of challenges. It was a pleasure to be involved in an extracurriculair program, in which many students were motivated to create the sustainable car of the future. The student team consisted of team mechanical, team electrical and software, team external relations and team design. Interdiscplinary communication can be challenging, but by working closely together it is a most powerfull tool, and a vital skill.
